Data from Sea Around Us on fish catch in the South China Sea show that after sharp increases since the 1950s, fish catch has remained relatively stagnant since the mid-1990s. This stagnation, despite continuing increases in fishing effort, indicates that fish stocks are overexploited.

Along with an overall increase in fishing effort, the way that most fish in the South China Sea are caught has changed over time. Fishing can be divided into four types: artisanal traditional fishing that uses relatively small, low-powered boats , industrial fishing that requires large vessels that make longer trips farther out and catch larger volumes of fish , recreational fishing for sport or pleasure , and subsistence fishing for personal, family, and community consumption or sharing
