The following is a list of partnerships that the CSIS research team analyzed for this report.

The partnerships selected aim to improve the productivity of farms and farmers’ incomes while enhancing food security. The CSIS research team conducted desk research, interviewed a series of experts, and held three private roundtables to gather data and inputs on the lessons learned from these partnership experiences. The research team considered recent partnerships, mainly those launched after 2010.


African Cocoa Initiative – World Cocoa Foundation, U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID), and IDH (2011)

  • ACI seeks to improve the livelihoods of cocoa producers in West Africa by reducing barriers to cocoa production and improving sustainability. As part of this partnership between the World Cocoa Foundation, USAID, and IDH, farmers in Ghana, Nigeria, Cameroon, and Cote d’Ivoire are provided with access to training programs, agro-chemicals, and cutting-edge processing technologies over a multi-year period.

AgrInvest: Enabling Inclusive and Efficient Private Sector Investment in Agri-Food Systems, 2019

  • This PPP aims to support private investor engagement with sustainable agricultural producers in Burkina Faso, Ethiopia, Kenya and Niger, with a particular emphasis on connecting rural farmers with blended finance investment. A partnership between the UN’s Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), Food Systems for the Future, the Dutch Entrepreneurial Development Bank, the European Centre for Development Policy Management, the government of Italy, and several other public and private stakeholders, the program aims to shore up small and medium-sized farmers in the face of a $2.5 trillion aid shortfall.

The Alliance for Inclusive and Nutritious Food Processing, 2018

  • The Alliance for Inclusive and Nutritious Food Processing (AINFP) is a joint initiative between Partners in Food Solutions, USAID, and TechnoServe aiming to fund the establishment of food processing centers and increase access to nutritious foods in Kenya, Ethiopia, Zambia, and Tanzania. Begun in 2018, AINFP seeks to expand cooperation between producers and processors and support processing capacity building to minimize the prevalence of hunger in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Challenge Fund for Youth Employment: Bayseddo - Connecting Senegalese farmers to investors, 2019

  • Through the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs’ Challenge Fund for Youth Employment, Bayseddo, an agro-tech startup, aims to bring together local farmers and outside investors through online platforms. Bayseddo seeks to utilize the digital space to optimize the investment-to-producer pipeline and reduce the cost of food.

Niger Delta Partnership Initiative, 2010

  • The Niger Delta Partnership Initiative (NDPI) Foundation and its sister organization the Partnership Initiatives in the Niger Delta (PIND), both funded by Chevron, aim to improve the lives and livelihoods of local communities in Nigeria. Among its many programs, the NDPI seeks to assist farmers in increasing productivity through capacity-building training and market penetration efforts.

Enterprise EthioPEA, 2011

  • As a joint initiative between USAID, WFP, PepsiCo, the PepsiCo Foundation, the government of Ethiopia's Ministry of Agriculture, and the Agricultural Transformation Agency, Enterprise EthioPEA aims to assist local producers in increasing their foodstuff yields while minimizing their water usage. Enterprise EthioPEA seeks to achieve these outcomes through training programs focused on improving local irrigation techniques and educating farmers on other cutting-edge agricultural practices.

Hatching Hope: Accelerating Incomes Through Diversification, 2019

  • The PPP aims to improve the livelihoods of small-scale poultry farmers in Kenya, Mexico, and India by providing them with market access, training, and resources. Through this global initiative, Heifer, Cargill, and Walmart work with local partners to provide small-scale farmers with the knowledge and tools necessary to start and sustain their own businesses, including training on animal welfare, nutrition, and business management.

Market-Oriented Smallholders Value Chain (MSVC Thailand), 2016

  • MSVC Thailand is a PPP that brings together the German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ), Olam Agri, Crop Life, and the government of Thailand. MSVC Thailand aims to support efforts to improve the sustainability of Thailand’s rice production and increase the livelihoods of the country’s many smallholder farmers. This initiative sought to achieve these outcomes through capacity-building programs and financial assistance for Thai rice producers.

The Partnership for Inclusive Agricultural Transformation in Africa (PIATA-AGRA), 2017

  • PIATA- AGRA aims to increase the livelihoods and productivity of African farmers through support from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation; the UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office (FCDO); the Rockefeller Foundation; USAID; and BMZ. With the backing of these organizations, PIATA-AGRA aims to support smallholder farmers through seed grants and capacity-building training programs.

Public-Private Partnership for Integrated Agricultural Development Initiative in India (PPP-IAD), 2015

  • PPP-IAD aims to bolster private investment in smallholder farms in India as part of efforts to increase foodstuff yields and producer incomes. Funded by the World Economic Forum and India’s Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, PPP-IAD aims to achieve these objectives by working with private sector partners to assist local producers in accessing improved irrigation technologies and reducing barriers to credit.

The Southern Agricultural Corridor of Tanzania (SAGCOT), 2010

  • SAGCOT aims to increase producer incomes and reduce the prevalence of poverty in Tanzania through PPPs focused on agriculture. SAGCOT aimed to achieve this these objectives by enlisting the help of AGRA, WEF, FAO, the World Bank, the Nature Conservancy, the government of Tanzania, several private sector companies, farmers organizations, implementing partners, foundations, research organizations, and public financial institutions.

Noble Argentina Timbues Expansion, 2017

  • Noble Argentina Timbues Expansion seeks to help Argentina’s soybean industry through an expansion of its soybean crushing facilities. This PPP aimed to increase the country’s soybean and soy-derivative production and export as well as raise the incomes of agricultural workers. This initiative was supported by the International Finance Corporation (IFC), ICC, Rabobank, and Renova S.A.

Partnership to Empower Women in the Potato Supply Chain in West Bengal, 2022-2023

  • This PPP sought to aid efforts to empower women food producers and aimed to achieve its objectives through seed grants and capacity-building programs for smallholder farmers. This program was supported by USAID, PepsiCo Foundation, PepsiCo, and Resonance Global.
